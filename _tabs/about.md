## How I Approach This

Before I touch anything in a new client account, I read it first.

Not the summary metrics. The actual data. I want to know where the money is going. Which spend is generating revenue? Which is generating nothing? What does the structure look like underneath the numbers?

Only after I understand what's actually happening do I start forming opinions about what needs to change.

I learned this the hard way. Auditing hundreds of Amazon ad accounts, I found that the symptoms were almost never the real problem.

I remember one audit where Broad Match keywords were outperforming Exact Match on both CTR and conversion. That usually shouldn't happen in a well-structured account. Exact Match is supposed to be your most controlled, most relevant targeting. Finding the opposite meant something was wrong upstream.

The fix wasn't in the campaigns at all. It was in the product listing.

I bring that same outlook into AI Engineering. When something doesn't behave the way I expect, my first instinct isn't to find a workaround. It's to understand why it's not working. That takes longer. But it builds the kind of understanding that actually sticks.

## Why I Write

I'm going to be honest. I hate writing.

Back in college, I dreaded every essay and term paper. I procrastinated until the last possible minute. Writing felt like a chore I needed to get through.

Then my thesis adviser assigned me a topic on bioethanol production optimization. Something shifted. I got really interested. I spent countless hours in the library researching the topic.

When it was time to write the proposal I had so many ideas and things to write I couldn't stop. My thesis adviser was impressed. After my presentation he told me that I have the potential to be great at whatever I put my heart into.

That simple message stuck with me until now.

I realized that when I love thinking through the topics I actually care about, writing becomes the natural way to work through it. And suddenly it doesn't feel like a chore anymore. It feels like part of the process.

AI Engineering is giving me that same feeling the bioethanol thesis gave me back then. So I write. Not because I'm a writer. Because I'm someone who found a subject worth thinking hard about.
